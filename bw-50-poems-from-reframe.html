50 Poems from ReFrame

a spontaneous poetry project by ben wolfe who may be reached via email. PDF here.

small stories
found and seen in the words and images of  peterborough’s documentary film festival, january 23-25, 2015

the poems range from entirely ‘found,’ to entirely written to something in between; they were posted live to social media throughout the festival

01

Once again I fall through the gates of sadness
I write on the board with chalk,
I seek the dreams I had as a child
I cannot find the words

02

How is life going?
Are your poems for the war
Or against the war
Or about the war?

03

The children draw lines
In the time line,
Born
Primary school,
The year (the day)
They left home
And had to start this new story
In this unfamiliar tongue

04

She has a dream
Of what she’d like to become
She does not have the training
She does not have the language
She does not know the way

05

If you had asked before the match
Everyone would have said no chance

Believe in your chance
They are still in the tournament
While Manchester United is out

06

It’s still our favourite café
We go out in our spare time,
Write poems…

07

“What if you don't repay the money
In six months?”
“They'll take my parents’ land.”

08

It's not just a test of right or wrong

It’s
How am I doing
What else must I learn ¿

09

Wearing their masks
They are allowed to pull the cart
In this parade

Everyone wants a picture,
’Til they see their face

10

Of course we had language problems at the start

School is out now
I’m not worried about you
You will find your way

11

Tonight is for the trees
The alley cats
The turning stars

And this young poet
Shining from a trail
Of words she wrote
When she was far from home

(the first lines are fragments of a spoken word piece by local poet sasha patterson)

12

Director's talk

Jealously, selfishly
I made this film
(And shared it here with you)

13

She was never really a fan
Of suburban apartment complexes —

Beating community hearts
Can’t be arbitrarily located,
Like shopping malls

Their only service is a parking lot
Five minutes walk won't get you very far

14

The perfect moment
For your change to bloom ¿

Wait ’til the by-law inspectors
Are on strike

15

Old visionaries never die
Their words return
As voice-overs to pictures they will never see

As those who heard
Turn dream
To documentary

16

My village, Zàbalo
I am the first one to be sent out
My tribe’s experiment

17

The cancer water flows beside the road
The children have no shoes

18

The truth is that we are already dead
Contamination in the rivers, in the streams

We cannot drink the water from the well
Or light a fire for fear the air will burn

19

This us our home
The land where we have lived
There is no other place for us to go

20

Ayahuasca, once or twice a year —
Ten years of psychotherapy in a night

21

I don't go with him
When he hunts at night

Too dangerous —
He comes back home at dawn
An animal across his back

He says "I'm hungry" —
Then he goes to sleep

22

Here no one tells us where to build our house
Or how

If it falls down
I guess that is our fault

23

Some films are poetry,
This one is prose

24

“Idle? Know more.”
“Treaty rights, not greedy whites…”

100 years of waiting.
This is about stories:

If you don't give it life
A story stops

25

“Cede, release, surrender and yield up
Forever, rights and land —”

What if you learned, a generation on
These treaty words
That no-one ever spoke

When you were asked to sign

26

“That is the whole objective of this Bill
No Indian left, who has not been absorbed”

Our policy, our government, our shame

What if we all,
Beginning where we are,
Said “Treaty people”
And learned what it means

27

I walked
Because we're struggling with our lives

So many
Have committed suicide
Five years ago I almost joined the lost

The youth here show
You can do anything

28

i ⋅ transit

‘Walking is medicine
You get to feel the land’
‘I feel our ancestors are with us here’

ii ⋅ arrival

16 years old — young eagle soars above
They'll tell their grandchildren about this day

29

A starship out in space
A multi-ethnic crew
He says he put to rest
That stereotype about Asian drivers
He was the best helmsman in the galaxy

30

Two soldiers with bayonets
Took us from our home
A train into the swamps of Arkansas

They taught us to pledge allegiance to the flag
Behind barbed wire and towers and pointed guns

An irony I did not understand

31

When I came out, my mom said
“So am I”

Turned out my aunts
Were really ‘special friends’

32

The first time was terrifying,
And delicious

After a while I
Remembered delicious more

33

(I helped introduce the quite wonderful film ‘To Be Takei’ on behalf of the Unitarian Fellowship of Peterborough, and included this haiku)

open hearts and minds
laughter and love are holy
proud to sponsor this

34

This bouncing, singing, dancing show-off child —
My parents knew
That they were saddled with a ham

35

If you take the hands off a clock,
Is it still keeping time ¿

36

150,000 photos
No one ever saw

A mystery
As haunting as her art

37

I make no provision in my will
For any of my relatives

For reasons known to me

That I disclosed
To a few closest friends

38

She liked headlines
That revealed the folly of humanity

I think they let her say
‘I told you so’

39

We used to laugh
(Behind his back)
Because he always would
Reuse his teabag

Sometimes he'd use
One bag
For days on end

40

“What are we going to do
With all these bacon bits?
That's gross.”

“Just take them all.
We'll put them on, like
Everything.”

41

These poems
Are photos from a whirlwind tour

Each film a country
Travelled in an hour

The words are keys
That open memory

42

Funeral trade show

Diamonds from DNA
Or we can blast your ashes into space —

This is the green pavilion
You can tell by the green carpet

43

Our council's held together
By bubble gum, and
Popsicle sticks

Doing work
That governments
In other countries do

44

This is my only picture
With my first mother,
I don't remember her :

I thank you God
For Mary
Our mother
Who gave us life

I ask you God to forgive her
For taking her own life

I ask you to forgive
All those who blame
And scapegoat her

45

Yet to be seen
Known only by God —

This night
Has that much meaning to me

46

You sent me on a journey
I didn't know existed

47

I really like it there
I love that tree

I know the view doesn't matter
When you're down so deep

But
I
Really
Like
It
There

48

I am not
The readings on the monitor

I am “I love you”
You can barely hear

In a phone message
You replay and play,

This song I still can sing
Behind this mask

With you,
As without pity
Fear or shame

You wash my feet

49

I feel very alive
When speaking about burials

50

I once sang
I once danced
I made music with my life

51

This silence

And then through our hands
Our love

52

The hard earth echoes
As we fill your grave

53

I want you to meet my girlfriend
She has beautiful German eyes
She has one beautiful German eye

54

It's time to do prostrations
Time to bow to life
Time to build a temple with a bow

55

I thought desperately
About impermanence

It's what one thinks
When seated on a throne

56

I wish
I were
Just starting
Now

But what can I do

57

Do something.
Do something.
Do something. (a fragment from Susan Sontag's notebook)
