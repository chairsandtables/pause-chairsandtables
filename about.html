Who We Are

The best way to get a chair at the table is to build one yourself.

Asad Chishti and Jonathon Reed are explorers.

Born in the Fall, a few years and several thousand kilometers apart, following their twin sisters, they ended up at the same university; Asad is passionate about potlucks, maps, and naps, and Jonathon about breaking down gender norms, ukuleles, and education. Between the two of them they've worked on projects with/for the Occupy Movement, Idle No More, Humans of Kingston, Vanhawks, TEDxQueensU, Awesome Kingston, Queen's Journal, CFRC, Jack.org, Ontario Youth Parliament, Adjacent Possibilities, MaRSDD, and more.

Chairs and Tables (CHAT) is a project we plan to work on till ~2024. Our North Star project is a multilayered-onion-like-map for Canada's 150th which will have layers driven by archives, data, audio, video, images, and more. Some of our projects are Explore (a time capsule), Finding Yourself Lost (a podcast), TU (a university), and we've got a grand total of 16 active projects which are slowly being sculpted. They range from tiny to large*. We're fun to work with and have lots of feathers and caps, so get in touch. *For example, we pick an annual theme (Happiness, Home) to research for four seasons and write a report on. The current annual theme is Health and we're looking for at least 131 guests to our digital-publishing-potluck.
