Who We Are

The best way to get a chair at the table is to build one yourself.

Asad Chishti and Jonathon Reed are explorers.

Born in the Fall, a few years and several thousand kilometers apart, following their twin sisters, they ended up at the same university; Asad is passionate about potlucks, maps, and naps, and Jonathon about breaking down gender norms, ukuleles, and education. Between the two of them they've worked on projects with/for the Occupy Movement, Idle No More, Humans of Kingston, Vanhawks, TEDxQueensU, Awesome Kingston, Queen's Journal, CFRC, Jack.org, Ontario Youth Parliament, Adjacent Possibilities, MaRSDD, and more.

The North Star for Chairs and Tables (CHAT) is a mapping project for Canada's 150th. Current active projects Explore (a time capsule), Finding Yourself Lost (a podcast), TU (a university). They range from tiny to large*.

*We pick an annual theme (Happiness, Home) to research for four seasons and write a report on. The current annual theme is Health and we're looking for guests to our digital potluck.
